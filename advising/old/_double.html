The Bachelors of Science in Computer Science (CoS) and the Bachelors of Science in Mathematics (CAS)

Considering the major requirements for both Computer Science and Mathematics, we note that CS__ 151_ and MATH 251W are required for both majors. Under the CoS policy to allow MATH-prefixed courses as valid Computer Science electives, this results in the following:

    CS__ 151_ MATH 251W MATH 446* MATH 499W MATH 400+

    • MATH 446_ xor MATH 456_ is a major requirement for Mathematics and an elective for Computer Science.

  • 4 courses are counted toward uniquely toward the Computer Science major, all as major requirements:
    CS__ 152_ CS__ 261_ CS__ 271_ CS__ 351_

  • 5 courses are counted toward uniquely toward the Mathematics major:
    MATH 253_ MATH 200+ MATH 200+ MATH 300+ MATH 300+
    • MATH 253_ is a major requirement
    • All others are electives counted solely toward the Mathematics major.

As with Computer Science and Data Science, we may think of this as on a Computer-Science-to-Mathematics gradient. As the major in Mathematics has lacks clear separation between required courses and electives, we will consider only whether courses count, rather than how they count.
