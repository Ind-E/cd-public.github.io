Satisfying Arrangements

Bachelors of Science in Computer Science and Data Science, CoS

Considering the major requirements for both Computer Science and Data Science, we note that CS__ 151_ and DATA 352W are required for both majors. So each major requires 5 unique courses and shares 2 courses, totaling 12. These 12 unique required courses form a 12-course double major in Computer Science and Data Science wherein courses required for one major, but not another, may be counted as electives for the second major. Of note, CS__ 271_ is not counted toward the Data Science major, though is a valid Data Science elective, and DATA 151_ and MATH 280_ * are not counted toward the Computer Science major, though are valid Computer Science electives.

Of note, Data Science majors will necessarily take a writing-centered course to fulfill their non-major graduation requirements, as two are required for general education requirements and only one, DATA 352W, is required to complete the major. Computer Science majors and double majors satisfy this requirement with MATH 251W. The marginal cost of a Computer Science major to an prospective Data Science major who has completed no electives is 1 course.

Looking forward, 3 of these courses can count toward the Mathematics major. CS__ 151_ and MATH 251W are major requirements for Mathematics, and MATH 280_ may count toward the 200-level elective requirement.

Bachelors of Science in Computer Science and Data Science (CoS) and Bachelors of Science in Mathematics (CAS)

Considering the major requirements for both Computer Science and Mathematics, we note that CS__ 151_ and MATH 251W are required for both majors. Considering the major requirements for both Data Science and Mathematics, we note that CS__ 151_ and MATH 280_ are required for both majors.

While CS__ 151_ fulfills a major requirement for all three majors, we may exercise the narrow distribution in the Mathematics major to count another Computer Science course numbered 200 or higher toward the requirement, such as CS__ 271_.

We can represent this across the three majors on a cyclic gradient. In the special case of Computing and Data Programs where a course is a major requirement for one major and an elective for another major, take "greater than" such as CS__>DATA to denote, in this case, Computer Science major requirement and a Data Science elective. For simplicity, conflate electives and requirements for the Mathematics major.
