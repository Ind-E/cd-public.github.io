Data Governance?

Data governance is a methodology that ensures data is in the proper condition to support business initiatives and operations.

  • Justify funding for the data governance program
  • Motivate participation by the business communities
  • Drive the priority of data governance activities
  • Drive the level of data integration required across participating business areas
  • Help to determine the right operating model, especially the level of centralization and decentralization required

How do you feel about data governance being considered part of data science? Is supporting business the responsibility of data scientists? Who benefits from this expectation?

Analytics Governance

  • Analytics governance involves governing data for use in analytic applications and usage of analytics systems.
  • The analytics governance team can establish governance mechanisms, such as:
    • Analytics report versioning.
    • Documentation standards.
  • It is essential to:
    • Keep track of regulatory requirements.
    • Establish company policies regarding analytics usage.
    • Provide guardrails to ensure compliance across the organization.

Who benefits from analytics governance?

Why?

  • According to Gartner, by 2025, 80% of organizations aiming to scale digital business may fail due to inadequate modern data and analytics governance.
  • Chief Data Officers (CDOs) regard data governance as a top priority for their initiatives.
  • In a 2023 survey by MIT CDOIQ, 45% of Chief Data Officers identified data governance as a critical concern.
  • Data leaders aim to establish a governance model ensuring:
    • Timely availability of data to the right people and applications.
    • Data security and safety with appropriate controls.

Machine Learning (ML) Governance

  • ML governance applies data governance practices to ML, including:
    • Ensuring data quality and integration for model training and deployment.
    • Responsible AI usage, especially concerning sensitive data.
    • Enabling participation in model building, deployment, and monitoring.
    • Documenting model training, versioning, supported use cases, and ethical guidelines.
    • Monitoring models in production for accuracy and potential issues like drift, overfitting, and underfitting.

Main Challenges

  • The strategic challenge for data governance is aligning the program with business initiatives:
    • Directly proposing the value of data governance can be ineffective.
    • Position data governance to support business initiatives.
    • Ensure data is in the right condition to support business success.
    • Do not overlook reporting and auditing practices for supporting initiatives.

Styles of Data Governance

  • Centralized data governance:
    • Centralize responsiblity for mission statements, policies, tool choices, and more.
    • The day-to-day actions are many times pushed into lines of business (LOB).
  • Federated data governance:
    • Individual business units operate in the way that best matches their needs.
    • A smaller centralized team which focuses their work on solving common problems, like making data quality tools.
  • Decentralized data governance:
    • Each LOB does what they need for their specific project.
    • Each project freely uses any tools or processes from other projects.
    • As approaches data mesh (2019-) increase in popularity, so does decentralized..

Who Builds Data Governance?

Building a business-centric data governance program requires many job functions.

  • Executives control funding to determine priorities for data governance support.
  • Data stewards monitor details of projects day to day. They elevate irregularities up to executives.
  • Data owners make policies about the data, including who should have access to the data and under what circumstances, how to interpret and apply regulations, and key term definitions
  • Data engineers are computing specialists, and provide tools that help secure data, manage data quality, integrate data from a variety of sources, and find the right data.

Data scientists can be any of these roles. Who does this leave out?

How does data governance work?

Data governance requires people, process, and technology solutions across a range of capabilities.

Curating Data at Scale

Data governance requires curating data at scale to limit data sprawl and manage valuable data sources effectively.

  • Identify and manage valuable data sources such as databases, data lakes, and warehouses.
  • Ensure accuracy, freshness, and integrity of data.
  • Eliminate sensitive information to instill confidence in data-driven decisions.

Capabilities include data quality management, data integration, and master data management.

Understanding Data in Context

Discovering and understanding data in context accelerates data-driven decisions and enhances business value.

  • Facilitate data discovery and comprehension for all users.
  • Centralize data catalog for easy access and usage.
  • Enable users to make informed business decisions based on data insights.

Capabilities include data profiling, data lineage, and data catalogs.

Protecting Data with Confidence

Protecting and securely sharing data is crucial, striking the right balance between privacy, security, and accessibility.

  • Implement controls for data privacy and security.
  • Govern data access across organizational boundaries.
  • Provide intuitive tools for both business and engineering users.

Capabilities include data lifecycle management, data compliance, and data security.

Reducing Business Risk

Reducing business risk and ensuring regulatory compliance are paramount, requiring monitoring and auditing of data access and usage.

  • Monitor data access and usage to mitigate risks.
  • Audit data access, including machine learning models, for security and compliance.
  • Ensure responsible use of data and simplify reporting processes.

Capabilities include usage auditing for data and machine learning.

How to Optimize?

Understands which data domains, sources, and elements are needed to supported by funders.

  • Build a data governance roadmap that shows support for a central mission, including overlaps.
  • Identify applications, considering requirements for freshness and privacy.
  • Understand what fit-for-purpose data looks like.
  • Sustain and expand the data governance program by embedding it in the enterprise operating model.
  • Organize the analytics community for self-service and consistency.
  • Support artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML).

Align with Business Initiatives

To ensure the effectiveness of your data governance program, consider aligning it with existing business initiatives:

  • Attach data governance initiatives to funded business projects to secure support and resources.
  • Develop a comprehensive data governance roadmap that aligns with targeted business initiatives.

Identify Data Requirements

Understanding the specific data needs of your organization's initiatives is crucial. Consider the following:

  • Determine applications and business intelligence scenarios requiring data support.
  • Consider factors such as data freshness and privacy in identifying data requirements.
  • Ensure that collected and managed data is fit for its intended purpose.

Integrate into Operating Model

Embedding data governance practices into your organization's operating model is essential for seamless operations:

  • Integrate data governance into existing processes and workflows.
  • Make data planning and implementation a natural part of daily operations.

Community Organization

Organizing the analytics community can promote self-service access to data and ensure consistency:

  • Facilitate self-service access to data within the analytics community.
  • Ensure consistency in data usage and interpretation across the organization.

Extend to AI and ML

Supporting artificial intelligence and machine learning initiatives requires extending data governance practices:

  • Extend data governance to cover the management of feature stores for AI and ML models.
  • Ensure that data governance supports the responsible use of sensitive data in AI and ML projects.

The "CIA" Triad

  • Confidentiality
  • Integrity
  • Availability

Introduced in "Post Processing Audit Tools and Techniques in 1977.

  • Go to link
  • Go to page 11-3
  • Alternatively, search "confidentiality". 
    OBJECTIVES OF A TYPICAL SECURITY AUDIT
The post processing activities of the auditor are presented here in the context of a security audit and include confidentiality, integrity and availability of data.

Confidentiality

  • The system must not disclose any information intended to be hidden
  • E.g. credit card information on a website
  • E.g. status of your accounts (to an unauthorized user)
  • Beware of indirect disclosure
    • Error messages can give away 'too much' information!
    • Info dumps when a page fails, or app fails
    • Login failures
    • URL query strings can tell you about internal data

Think: is open source software likely to be more or less confidential?

Integrity

  • The system must not allow assets to be subverted by unauthorized users
  • E.g. changing a student’s academic status
  • We must be able trust what is in the system
    • The data being stored
    • The functionality being executed
    • NOTE: Under the von Neumann model these are both just DATA

Think: what makes us willing to trust (1) something on the internet or (2) an email?

Availability

  • The system must be able to be available and operational to users
  • E.g. bringing down Amazon.com
  • These are extremely hard to protect against
  • Any system performance degradation that can be triggered by a user can be used for denial of service attacks
  • Concurrency issues, infinite loop, or resource exhaustion

Think: when was the last time Facebook was down? Netflix? Healthcare.gov?

Auditability or Nonrepudiation

  • An actor should not be able to deny or dispute their actions
  • E.g. instructors allowing cheat sheet usage on exam
  • A party of a transaction cannot deny having received a transaction, and the other party cannot deny having sent the transaction
