Discussion

In her wonderful short story, “The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas,” the late Portland-based writer Ursula K. Le Guin presents a challenge to the pure utilitarian model in which one tallies happiness and pain to find the greatest good for the greatest number. How effective did you find the story? If you were asked to defend the concept of utilitarianism, how would you respond to Le Guin’s story.
