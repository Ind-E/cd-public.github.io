PTML

To organize our thoughts, we introduce "Plain Text Markup Language"

PTML

  • Synthesizes words into sentences
  • Governed by English grammar
  • Readable by humans
  • Teaches writing

HTML

  • Synthesizes content into elements
  • Governed by HTML Living Standard
  • Renderably by browsers
  • Teaches data & computing

Both

  • Use HTML style tags

Process

To convert plain text to PTML we:

  1. Tag words (with syntactic categories)
  2. Identifying intermediate phrases (syntactic words to syntactic phrases)
  3. Re-type words if needed (e.g. verb to noun via gerund)
  4. Compose subjects, predicates, and conditionally objects into sentence

Can you automate this process?

Word Tags

To tag words we identify a word as one of:

  • Noun
  • Verb
  • Adjective
  • Adposition
  • Adverb
  • Conjunction

These categories seem sufficient to capture all writing controlling of certain tones.

Sentence Tags

Sentences are composed of either

  • Both a subject and a predicate, or
    • Subjects are composed of a
      • Noun or noun phrase
    • Predicates are composed of a
      • A verb or verb phrase, and potentially
      • An object - a noun or noun phrase.
  • A sentence, a conjunction, and another sentence.

There may be phrases for each type of word (e.g. adjective phrase) and objects

  • Adposition phrases and verb phrases for transitive verbs require objects, a type of noun phrase.

Phrases

Yawn

  • A noun phrase contains at least a noun and may contain adjectives or adjective phrases and adpositional phrases.
  • A verb phrase contains at least a verb and may contain an object, a special type of noun phrase, adverbs or adverb phrases, adpositional phrases.
  • An adjective phrase contains at least a adjective and may contain an adverb or adverb phrase.
  • An adposition phrase contains at least an adjective and an object, a special type of noun phrase.
  • An adverb phrase contains at least an adverb and may additionally contain another adverb or adverb phrase.
  • Any of these phrases may composed of multiple subphrases joined by a conjunction.
